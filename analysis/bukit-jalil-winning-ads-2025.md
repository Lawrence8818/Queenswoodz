## A. Executive Summary

Across the whole account, Lawrence's Bukit Jalil ad spend concentrates on three
projects — **Queens Woodz (QW)**, **Treewoodz (TW)**, and **Vela / Golf View** —
all marketed under one consistent positioning: *"the REAL Bukit Jalil address,
walking distance to Pavilion + LRT, big units for own-stay, from ~RM1,800/mth."*

**The single biggest takeaway:** the winning formula is **Price anchor + "real Bukit
Jalil" location proof + big-unit/own-stay framing + scarcity bonus + low-friction
CTA.** The one ad that nailed all five — `QW_Vid 1_Site` — absorbed **MYR10,982 over
8 straight months**, more than 3× any other single ad. Video + a hard price/size
headline + Chinese long-form storytelling consistently beat short English image ads
on *engagement*, while short English image ads (Treewoodz) won on *cheap lead volume*.

**Do more of:** Chinese long-form video, RM-per-month headline, "真正Bukit Jalil
(not Kinrara/BK5/Puchong)" differentiation, "walk to Pavilion + LRT", first-50/last-X
scarcity bonus (free 80% branded furniture / kitchen cabinet), and the agent's
"I'll survey every BJ project for you, no spam calls" trust hook.

**Second-pass (cost-per-lead) takeaway — §B2:** when you re-rank the *same* winners by
**how cheaply each bought a lead**, the order flips. Your biggest-spend hero video
(`QW_Vid 1_Site`) is only **mid-pack on CPL (RM62.76)** — it's a *volume* engine, not
your cheapest. The **cheapest leads come from short image "info-gate" ads** (Treewoodz
RM50.78, Vela RM54.90), and the **highest-CTR ad (`QW_Vid 3_VR`) is the *most expensive*
per lead (RM77.19)** — proof that **high CTR ≠ cheap leads**. Blended account benchmark:
**~RM61 / lead** (454 leads / MYR27.8k across the lead-objective winners).

**Third-pass (cost-per-SALE) takeaway — §B3:** when ranked by **actual units closed**
(29 buyers attributed across these ads), the picture sharpens again. The true ROI
champions were **small-budget ads** — `6_pic_Golf View` (RM647 → 2 of the *biggest*
units) and `Vela` (~RM512/unit → **6 sales**) — both *under-funded* in Pass 1. The Hero
`QW_Vid 1_Site` earns its big budget with **9 closed units** (RM1,220/unit). Critically,
**`QW Video 1` closed ZERO units despite RM3,360 spend and 56 "cheap" leads** — proof that
**cheap leads ≠ buyers**. Blended **~RM995 to acquire a buyer**. The investor angle
("升值空间 / passive income / 不用供") quietly closed units and is under-exploited.

**What changes when you rank by efficiency, not scale:**
- 🔑 **Cheapest leads = short image "info-gate" ads, not the hero video.** `Treewoodz
  2_Lead` (RM50.78) and the `Vela` image ad (RM54.90) beat *every* video on cost-per-lead.
  The minimalist *"register to unlock price/floorplans"* formula is your most cost-efficient
  lead engine — and it's cheap to produce.
- ⚠️ **Your #1 spend ad is only mid-pack on CPL.** `QW_Vid 1_Site` bought 175 leads (3× any
  other) but at **RM62.76 — ~24% dearer per lead than Treewoodz.** It earns its budget on
  **volume + 8-month stability**, not efficiency. Keep scaling it, but treat it as the
  *volume engine*, not the *cheapest source*.
- 🚩 **The highest-CTR ad is the WORST on cost-per-lead.** `QW_Vid 3_VR` (Golf View VR) had
  the best CTR (5.08%) in Pass 1 — yet at **RM77.19/lead it's the most expensive** of all
  winners. Its premium/narrow audience drives CPM to RM195, which eats the click advantage.
  **Lesson: high CTR ≠ cheap leads.** Use the VR video for **quality / retargeting**, not
  cheap top-funnel volume.

## C. Top 3 Deep Dive — Hook / Angle / CTA

### 🥇 #1 — `QW_Vid 1_Site` · Queens Woodz · MYR10,982 · 8 months
*Headline:* **"月供RM1,800+ | 最大1400sf 4房"** (From RM1,800/mth | up to 1400sf, 4 rooms)
*Format:* Vertical video · *CTA button:* **Sign Up** (lead form)

- **HOOK:** *"来了❗️ 你一定没有看过这个全新Bukit Jalil新项目"* — "It's here! You've
  definitely never seen this brand-new Bukit Jalil project." A curiosity + novelty
  open, reinforced by a hard **price-and-size headline** (RM1,800/mth, 1,400sf, 4 rooms)
  that filters for serious own-stay buyers in the first second.
- **ANGLE (stacked, 6 layers):**
  1. **Affordability anchor** — RM1,800/mth for the *biggest* unit.
  2. **Anti-compromise / own-stay** — "❌Studio ❌1-room ✅100% own-stay", 4 big rooms.
  3. **Location proof** — 5 steps to Link 1, 5 min to Pavilion + 海底捞, 300m walk to LRT,
     2 stops to Stadium Axiata (concerts, no traffic jam).
  4. **Scarcity / exclusivity** — low density ("no 1,000 units"), privacy; **first 50 get
     unblocked view + early-bird + 80% branded furniture, quota-limited**.
  5. **Agent trust hook** — "I'll analyse *every* new KV/Bukit Jalil project for you, save
     you 80% of your time — no need to click ad after ad. #won't-spam-call-you."
  6. **FOMO close** — "anyone who knows Bukit Jalil knows this location is once-in-a-
     lifetime; miss it and it's gone."
- **CTA:** *"现在就点击联系我，我们Showroom见!"* — "Click now to contact me, see you at the
  showroom." Soft, personal, showroom-appointment framing (not "buy now").
- **Why it won:** Only ad that stacks *all* persuasion layers; video format kept CPM low
  (39) at huge scale; ran 8 straight months — Meta's algorithm had a stable, proven
  winner to optimise into. **This is the template to clone.**

### 🥈 #2 — Treewoodz "Sign Up for Viewing" · MYR6,879 combined · ~8 months across 2 relaunches
*Headline:* **"Sign Up for Viewing now"** · *Format:* Static image · *CTA:* **Sign Up**

- **HOOK:** *"Latest Development RIGHT NEXT to Pavilion Bukit Jalil 🏡"* — pure location
  adjacency, in English, one line.
- **ANGLE:** Minimalist **info-gating**. Only two facts given — *"Size up to 1500sf | 2–4
  Bedrooms"* + `#registerforfulldetails #updatedinfo`. Everything else (price, name,
  floorplans) is withheld to *force the lead form*. The opposite philosophy to #1.
- **CTA:** "Sign Up for Viewing now" — register to unlock details.
- **Why it won:** Cheapest path to *lead volume* — the curiosity gap + Pavilion anchor +
  low CPM (43) produced steady form fills cheaply, which is why Lawrence relaunched the
  exact same creative twice. English + short = broad reach; great top-of-funnel
  volume engine, complements #1's high-intent storytelling.

### 🥉 #3 — `QW_Vid 3_VR` · Golf View · MYR3,396 · **5.08% CTR** (best of the big spenders)
*Headline:* **"别乱买Bukit Jalil屋子,先看这个才决定!"** (Don't blindly buy a Bukit Jalil home —
see this first!) · *Format:* Video (VR walkthrough) · *CTA:* **Learn More**

- **HOOK:** A **warning / pattern-interrupt** — "Don't simply buy a Bukit Jalil unit,
  watch this before you decide." Challenges the buyer instead of selling, which drives
  the 5.08% CTR (2.5× the account norm).
- **ANGLE:** **Last-chance + premium view + space.** "Bukit Jalil's *last* [5-min-walk to
  LRT + Pavilion] Golf View large-layout condo", 1,000–1,400sf, up to 4 car parks; two
  master bedrooms; only 4 units per floor; private Golf + Park View; **last 3 get
  upgraded kitchen cabinet.** Scarcity + exclusivity + lifestyle.
- **CTA:** *"现在点击'Learn More'，优先帮你选最好的单位!"* — "Click Learn More, I'll prioritise the
  best unit for you." Help-framed, not push.
- **Why it won:** The interrupt/advice hook earns the click; VR video sells the premium
  Golf View that static images can't. High CPM (195) is the cost of a high-intent,
  smaller, qualified audience — efficient for *quality* over *volume*.

## D. Cross-Cutting Insights (what the winners share)

**Winning HOOK patterns (in order of proven strength):**
1. **Hard price + size headline** — "月供RM1,800+ | 最大1400sf 4房", "RM2.5k/mth | 1200–1900sf".
   The RM-per-month number is the most repeated, highest-spend hook in the account.
2. **"Real Bukit Jalil" differentiation** — "真正的Bukit Jalil地址! 不是Kinrara 也不是BK5… 不是
   Puchong" directly attacks competitors mis-selling fringe locations as "Bukit Jalil."
3. **Warning / pattern-interrupt** — "别乱买Bukit Jalil屋子,先看这个" → highest CTR.
4. **Novelty/curiosity** — "来了❗️你一定没看过这个全新项目".

**Dominant ANGLE:** *Own-stay, big-unit, walk-to-Pavilion-&-LRT affordability* — NOT
investment/rental-yield. Every top ad sells **lifestyle + space + location for a family
to live in**, anchored on a low monthly installment, with a **scarcity bonus**
(first-50 / last-3 free furniture or kitchen cabinet) to force urgency.

**Best CTA / funnel structure:**
- **Sign Up (lead form)** carries the highest-spend, top-of-funnel ads (#1, #2) →
  best for *volume*.
- **Learn More** pairs with the high-intent video walkthroughs (#3) → best for *quality*.
- **WhatsApp** appears on smaller retarget/warm ads (1_LLP_QW_Ai_Wa, BJ Address Big Size)
  → use for *bottom-funnel* close, not cold prospecting.
- Every CTA is **help-framed** ("I'll help you pick the best unit", "see you at the
  showroom", "no spam calls") — the agent-as-advisor positioning is itself a conversion lever.

**Format & longevity lessons:**
- **Video = scale + cheap CPM.** The two cheapest-CPM ads (Video_1_LxH 10.84, 11.62% CTR;
  Video_1_LxH_Latest 10.82, 10.39% CTR) are *educational* videos ("choosing the right
  location for family") — excellent cheap top-of-funnel reach engines.
- **Longevity is the strongest winner-signal:** #1 ran 8 unbroken months. Don't kill a
  working creative — refresh/relaunch it (as Treewoodz was) to reset fatigue.
- **Language split works:** Chinese long-form video = high-intent storytelling; short
  English image = cheap broad lead volume. Run both, for different funnel stages.

**Benchmark read (account norms):** typical CTR ~1.5–2.5%, CPM ~MYR40–90. Anything above
~3.5% CTR (QW_Vid 3_VR 5.08%, educational videos 10%+) is a hook worth scaling; CPM
above ~MYR120 is acceptable only when CTR/intent is high (Golf View VR).

   retargeting** (worst CAC, small units only).

  then scale the top 1–2 like `QW_Vid 1_Site`.
- **Creative mix:** 3 video + 2 image. Keep every winning ad live 30+ days before judging.
- **Always include:** an RM/month number in the headline, a "real Bukit Jalil + walk to
  Pavilion/LRT" line, and a quota-limited bonus.

### Quick scaling rules
1. Judge nothing before **30 days / ~50 leads**.
2. Scale the winner by **budget on the same ad** (like #1), don't recreate it.
3. When CTR drops ~30% from peak, **relaunch the same creative in a fresh ad set** (the Treewoodz move).
4. Keep **RM/month + "real Bukit Jalil" + walk-to-Pavilion/LRT + quota bonus** in every cold ad.
5. Reserve **WhatsApp CTA for retargeting** warm viewers, not cold traffic.
6. **Judge cold ads on cost-per-lead (~RM61 benchmark), not CTR.** A high-CTR ad can still
   be your most expensive lead source (Golf View VR = RM77). Scale on CPL; use CTR only to
   read hook strength.
7. **Lean image "info-gate" ads for cheap volume** (your CPL champions: Treewoodz RM50,
   Vela RM55), and **graduate the RM28–38 early-signal cuts** (`QW Video 3_VR General`,
   `QW Video 2_Ai`) into a RM50–80/day test before trusting them.
8. **The real scoreboard is cost-per-SALE, not cost-per-lead.** Tag every closed unit back
   to its source ad and rank by CAC (~RM995 blended benchmark; under that = scale, well
   over = fix). This is exactly what the CAPI lead-stage sync in `/capi` is built to
   automate — feed `deal_signed` back so Meta optimises toward *buyers*, not form-fills.
9. **Kill the zero-sale leakers fast.** Any ad that passes the 30-day / 50-lead mark with
   **0 closings (like `QW Video 1`) gets cut or re-offered** — cheap leads that never buy
   are the most expensive line on the account.
